Comments about the book:

- The goal of the book is to produce a Bourbaki-style reconstruction of real analysis paying close attention to foundations and motivations. 
- I am genuinely not sure who this book is for.
- I do not think this book would be pedagogically useful, although if anyone finds it helpful, that would be a plus. 

Comments about my choice of presentation:

- The development of the book involves several layers simultaneously. Instead of FOL + ZFC, I take FOL + ZFC + conceptual layer + occasionally syntactic layer. 
- I avoid invoking "..." notation unless it is justified beforehand. No strings (abstract or concrete) are ever taken to be infinite. 
- Although most of the book will be done at an informal level, everything ought to have a straightforward mapping to something formal at the base level. For example, the introduction of symbols (e.g. "Let...", "Define...") is tied to taking conservative extensions of our working theory. The ZFC axioms are only our initial axioms for our initial theory. 
- Partial functions are part of everyday mathematical practice, but in usual FOL and model theory, all function symbols must be globally defined, meaning that at the formal level, partial functions cannot exist. I needed to reconcile this with the way things done outside of logic without abandoning FOL (partial FOL or free logic stray too far away from the desired way of doing things). This is why I opted to invoke Wiedijk and Zwanenburg's formulation of FOL with Domain Conditions. This way, our work maps onto FOL and we can be honest about taking terms like "1/0" as truly undefined. 

About the process:

- This book is a work in progress. Many old sections near the start get re-edited and re-arranged constantly. 
- If anyone sees a mistake, issue, or something confusing, constructive criticism would be massively appreciated. 
- There is no doubt the draft will contain errors, typos, and massive conceptual problems. Again, pointing them out is very important.
